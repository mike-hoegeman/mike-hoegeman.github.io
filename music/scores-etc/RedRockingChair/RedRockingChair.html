Red Rocking Chair

This page is a guide to learning Red Rocking Chair on a tapping instrument such as the Chapman Stick or the acoustic Dragonfly DFA.

There is a score/tablature that will help you along the way. These tabs fingerings are for a 12 string matched reciprocal tuning. This song lives in a range however, that should be adaptable to other tapping instrument tunings.

Background

  • This song is by the prolific songwriter Traditional. As a result, there are many versions and it goes by other names as well. It was made popular in recent times by an adaptation by Doc Watson on his 1981 album of the same name
  • The one this transcription is loosely based on is by the pickup trio of Tim O'Brien, Bill Frisell, & Dale Bruning on their album Life Lessons which is was recorded (live?) at eTown Hall in Boulder Co. This version to me is a lot more interesting in it's move away from bluegrass to a slower tempo and folk-jazz-blues undercurrent that's more reminiscent of the song's Scottish Ballad origins. The lyrics are also more ambiguous which lends it a mysterious air.
  • Here's another version that's in the earlier original ballad style that is more similar to the transcription than other bluegrassy versions.

Musescore score + tabs

This is the Authoritative version which is for twelve string Matched reciprocal tuning.

There may be others in the future with different tunings.

Elemental Structure

The keys of this song have been various. This version is modal, in particular D Dorian which contributes in a significant way to it's aforementioned folk-jazz-blues feel.
