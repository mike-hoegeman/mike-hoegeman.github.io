Haflög (II. Gárur)

This page is a guide to learning Gulli Björnsson's song Gárur, a solo guitar piece, taken from a larger suite called Haflög (Ocean songs)

This version is arranged for a tapping instrument such as the Chapman Stick or the acoustic Dragonfly DFA. There is a score/tablature & fretboard diagrams, to help you along the way.

Gárur Takes its name from the small ripples that appear on the surface of still water, often signaling a subtle shift in wind or energy.

Some notes about the piece

  • The piece is minimalistic and shifts between 5/8 and 6/8
  • Each bar has a triplet repeated three times, played as an ascending argpeggio.
  • The bass note of each arpeggio is played on the bass side, the remaining notes on the melody side.

A strategy ... works for me anyways :)

  • Break the piece up into chunks (4 bars is what i do)
  • Take a few minutes (or however long it takes) to learn the bass note sequence and be comfortable with moving to each note in the sequence
  • One bar at a time add in the melody part of the arpeggiation shapes and mentally associate it with the bass line note.
  • Note: You can playback/loop on any set of bars at the piece's musescore web page , at whatever speed is comfortable as a learning aid.
