Ralph Towner's Goodbye Pork Pie Hat

This page is a guide to learning Goodby Pork Pie Hat on a tapping instrument such as the Chapman Stick or the acoustic Dragonfly DFA.

There is a score/tablature that will help you along the way. These tabs/fingerings are for a 12 string matched reciprocal tuning.

Background

  • This song is by Charles Mingus and is well seated within the pantheon of great jazz tunes.
  • This version is an arrangement by Ralph Towner for solo 12 string guitar. Ralph has recorded several different versions of this song. This arrangement is found on his album Anthem and is his shortest and most unusual one.
  • A spotify link to hear what the arrangement sounds like in the original.

Elemental Structure

There is a atmospheric A and C prologue/epilogue sections, with once through of the basic refrain as section B in the middle. This makes for a short approx 2 minute piece.

It's very different harmonic take on the song, as opposed to the blues based way the song takes normally. You make not even recognize it at first, it has a very impressionistic feel harmonically. It's very much in Towner's personal sensibility of arragements and original compositions.

Section A

This section is repetition of a 1-bar "finger-sticking" style phrase with open space at the end of the bar for variations.

The lick is harmonically a Emin7+sus4 chord interleaved like so:

↓ = bass side ↑ = melody side

( ↓ E3 ↑ E5 ↓ A3 ↑ G4 ↑ B4 ↑ D4 )
between the melody and bass sides in such a way that emphasise the suspended note nicely with the root E and A present as bass notes.
